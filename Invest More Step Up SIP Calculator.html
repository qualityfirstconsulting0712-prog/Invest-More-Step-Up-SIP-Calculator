Why Start a SIP?

Starting a SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) early helps you benefit from the power of compounding — small, consistent investments grow exponentially over time. It brings discipline, reduces timing risk (you invest every month), and smooths out market volatility. Plus, starting early means you need to invest less or take fewer risks to reach your goals.

Step‑Up SIP takes this further by increasing your monthly investment periodically (usually annually). This helps you keep pace with inflation, growing income, or changing goals. Over time, your investment contributions increase, your corpus grows more, and you get a stronger cushion against rising prices.

How Step‑Up SIP Works

  1. You select an initial monthly SIP amount you can afford now.
  2. You decide a step‑up rate (e.g. 5‑15%) to increase SIP each year.
  3. You choose expected annual return rate (nominal) and an inflation rate to compute real value.
  4. Each year, your monthly investment increases by the chosen step‑up percentage.
  5. Contributions are made every month; you get both nominal growth and inflation‑adjusted (real) growth, along with year‑wise visibility of invested vs returns.

How to Use

  • Enter the monthly SIP you can invest now.
  • Select how much you want to increase it annually (step‑up %).
  • Enter expected annual nominal return (based on mutual funds or your estimate) and inflation rate to adjust for real value.
  • Choose investment duration in years.
  • Click “Calculate” to see year/month wise table, nominal vs real corpus, and a pie chart showing invested vs returns.
